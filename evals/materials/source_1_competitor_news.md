# TechCrunch: Proptech startup HomeQual raises $38M Series B to scale AI-first buyer qualification for residential developers

October 14, 2024

Berlin-based HomeQual announced today a $38M Series B round led by Atomico, with participation from existing investors Balderton Capital and LocalGlobe. The round brings total funding to $61M.

HomeQual operates a marketplace-adjacent SaaS that sits between property developers and classified portals across Germany, Poland, and most recently Spain. Its core offering is a voice-AI agent — branded "Nora" — that handles first-line qualification of inbound buyer inquiries on behalf of developers. The company claims that since full rollout of Nora in Q1 2024:

- **Average handle time dropped 52%** on calls tagged by its routing as "information-seeking"
- **Qualified lead pass-through to human agents improved from 31% to 44%**
- Developers using the platform report a 23% increase in booked viewings per inbound lead, though HomeQual notes this figure is self-reported by four anchor customers
- Nora now handles ~62% of first-line calls across the German customer base

In the announcement, HomeQual CEO Lena Keller framed the product as "category-redefining": "Developers have historically been stuck with either low-touch classifieds funnels or expensive human-staffed call centers. We believe the future of new-development sales is a qualified-lead product that a developer can trust, backed by AI that can be tuned to their specific inventory, financing products, and buyer segments. We're not replacing agents — we're replacing the 40% of agent time that used to be spent on people who weren't ready to buy."

The company plans to use the funding to expand into two new markets in 2025 and build out developer-facing analytics dashboards. Early press coverage has been positive, with Sifted calling the round "one of the most interesting proptech bets in Europe this year."

Industry analyst Martin Obermayer (PropTech Insights) offered a more cautious take: "The handle-time numbers are impressive, but the qualified-lead improvement is where it gets interesting — and also where the data gets thinner. Four anchor customers is not a sample. And the underlying question — whether developers actually pay more for AI-qualified leads vs. human-qualified leads — hasn't been answered in any market I've seen. The unit economics story is more plausible on the cost side than the revenue side."

HomeQual did not disclose revenue or the split between subscription and per-lead pricing.

The company competes most directly with traditional classified portals that have added developer CRM tools (ImmoScout24 in Germany, OtoDom in Poland), and with a handful of local SaaS startups building similar AI-agent tooling. In the US, VoiceFlow Residential raised $22M in March on a similar premise, though it has stayed on the brokerage side rather than the developer side.

Keller noted that HomeQual has no immediate plans to expand into the US or Asia: "Our thesis is specific to markets where new-development sales flow through classified portals rather than MLS-style broker networks. That's most of continental Europe, parts of Eastern Europe, and large parts of Asia and Latin America. The US is a different animal."
